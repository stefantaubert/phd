Comparison of implicit and explicit phoneme duration learning and its impact on intelligibility and naturalness of neural speech synthesis

Stefan Taubert

Abstract: Speech synthesis (text-to-speech, TTS) is the computer-assisted conversion of text into speech, with intelligibility and naturalness being the most important quality criteria. Neural approaches have significantly improved speech quality in recent years, but do not yet offer precise control of duration at the phoneme level. This thesis investigates whether a neural TTS system should explicitly learn to control duration at the phoneme level during training, or whether learning implicitly from extensive speech data is sufficient. To this end, an existing TTS system was extended to learn explicitly by automatically annotating duration. The pipeline included phonemic transcription of the recordings, temporal alignment, and partitioning of phoneme durations into four quantiles. A subjective evaluation via Amazon Mechanical Turk showed that the explicit approach resulted in a highly significant improvement in naturalness while maintaining intelligibility. Objective measurements confirmed the qualitative superiority of the explicit approach. The method increases the controllability and naturalness of neural speech synthesis and opens up new directions for linguistic research.

Audio samples

Recommendation: You will get the best listening experience with headphones.

All files were rated by at least twelve people, with at least two representatives from each demographic group (age + gender):

  • Age: 18-29 / 30-49 / 50+.
  • Gender: male / female.

Training and synthesis (acoustic model) were done using tacotron-cli. waveglow-cli was used with a pre-trained model from Nvidia to synthesize waveforms from the mel-spectrograms (vocoder). Acoustic models are published on Zenodo:

  • Explicit model, trained on these transcriptions.
  • Implicit model was trained on the same transcriptions but with duration markers removed.

Results: The explicit model achieved a significant improvement in naturalness (p=0.0014, n=33) compared to the implicit model while maintaining the same level of intelligibility.

  • MOS naturalness (explicit vs. implicit): 3.55 ± 0.27 vs. 3.49 ± 0.28 (GT: 4.17 ± 0.23).
  • MOS intelligibility (explicit vs. implicit): 4.44 ± 0.24 vs. 4.43 ± 0.21 (GT: 4.63 ± 0.19).

The explicit model can be used in the browser on HuggingFace via stefantaubert/en-tts.
